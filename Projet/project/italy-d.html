1) Affogato

Affogato is a traditional Italian dessert that consists of a shot of hot espresso poured over a scoop of vanilla gelato or ice cream. The combination of hot and cold creates a unique flavor and texture. It is often served as a simple, yet decadent after-dinner treat.

2) Amaretti

Amaretti are traditional Italian macaroon-style cookies made with almond flour, sugar, and egg whites. They have a crisp, crunchy texture and a sweet, almond flavor. They are often served with coffee or after-dinner drinks and are used in various dessert recipes, such as biscotti and Tiramisu.

3) Anginetti

Anginetti are Italian cookies that are traditionally round or oval-shaped, and often have a lemon or orange flavor. They are often dusted with powdered sugar and are a popular treat for special occasions, such as Easter and Christmas.

4) Babà Napoletano

Babà Napoletano is a type of Italian dessert that originated in Naples. It's a small, round cake made from a yeasted dough that's soaked in rum syrup, giving it a moist and spongy texture. The cake is typically served in small portions, either plain or topped with a fruit syrup or whipped cream. It's a sweet and boozy treat that's popular in Italian cafes and pastry shops.

5) Bignè di San Giuseppe

Bignè di San Giuseppe, also known as Saint Joseph's cream puffs, is a traditional Italian pastry that is typically served on Saint Joseph's Day, a Catholic holiday. It's made of a choux pastry dough that's piped into small, round puffs and then baked until golden and crisp. The puffs are then filled with a sweet whipped cream or pastry cream filling and sometimes topped with powdered sugar or a chocolate drizzle. These light and airy treats are a popular dessert in Italian cuisine.

6) Panettone

panettone, cake of Italian origin that is traditionally associated with Christmas and New Year festivals. Enriched with butter, eggs, sugar, raisins, candied fruit, and lemon or orange zest, panettone is essentially the Italian Christmas cake, eaten during the festive period including the Christmas and New Year holidays. So popular is it that the country produces more than 7,000 tons of panettone every year, about 10 percent of which is sold for export.

7) Bussolai

Bussolai are a type of traditional Italian biscuit from the island of Elba. They are round, sweet biscuits made from a simple dough of flour, sugar, and olive oil, and often flavored with aniseed. Bussolai have a crisp texture and a slightly crunchy exterior, and are often enjoyed with a glass of sweet dessert wine. They are a popular snack and are often served as a sweet treat with coffee or tea.

8) Cannoli

Cannoli are a classic Italian dessert that consist of a crisp, tube-shaped pastry shell filled with a sweet ricotta cheese filling, often flavored with chocolate chips, cinnamon, and candied fruit. The filled shells are usually dusted with powdered sugar before serving. Cannoli originated in Sicily and have become a staple of Italian-American cuisine. They are a popular treat for special occasions and holidays, and are often served with coffee or after-dinner drinks.

9) Cantuccini & Vin Santo

Cantuccini al Vin Santo is a traditional Italian dessert that consists of Cantuccini biscuits served with Vin Santo wine. Cantuccini are crunchy, almond-flavored biscuits that are baked twice to create a firm texture. Vin Santo is a sweet, amber-colored wine that is made from a blend of white grape varieties and is traditionally aged for several years in small oak barrels. The combination of Cantuccini and Vin Santo is a popular after-dinner treat in Italy and is often served as a dessert with coffee or tea. The biscuits are typically dipped in the Vin Santo before being eaten, which softens the crunchy texture and enhances the flavors of both the biscuits and the wine.

10) Cassata

Cassata is a traditional Italian dessert that originated in Sicily. It's a round cake made from layers of sponge cake and ricotta cheese filling, and is often decorated with candied fruit and covered in a bright green marzipan. The cake is typically chilled in the refrigerator to allow the flavors to meld and the texture to firm up. Cassata is a popular treat for special occasions and holidays, and is often served as a dessert with coffee or after-dinner drinks.

11) Crostata

Crostata is an Italian dessert tart that consists of a shortbread-like pastry crust filled with sweet preserves, fruit, or custard. The filling is covered with a lattice-patterned layer of pastry that is often decorated with additional fruit or sprinkled with sugar. Crostata is a versatile dessert that can be made with a variety of fillings, and is popular in both sweet and savory preparations. It's a staple of Italian cuisine and is often served as a dessert with coffee or after-dinner drinks.

12) Gelato

Gelato is a traditional Italian frozen dessert that is similar to ice cream, but is made with a different production process and contains less air, resulting in a denser and creamier texture. It's made with a mixture of milk, sugar, and flavorings, which are slowly churned at a lower speed and temperature compared to ice cream. This results in a denser, smoother product that is typically served at a slightly warmer temperature than ice cream. Gelato comes in a variety of flavors, from classic favorites like chocolate and vanilla to more unique and seasonal flavors. It's a popular treat in Italy and around the world, and is often enjoyed as a refreshing dessert on a hot day.

13) Granita

Granita is a traditional Italian frozen dessert that is similar to sorbet but has a coarser, grainy texture. It's made by freezing a mixture of sugar, water, and flavorings, and then scraping the mixture with a fork to create a rough, icy texture. The most popular flavor of Granita is lemon, but it can also be made with other fruits such as orange, strawberry, and melon. It's a refreshing treat that is often enjoyed in the hot summer months and is often served as a dessert with coffee or after-dinner drinks.

14) Maritozzo

Maritozzo is a traditional Italian sweet bread that is typically served as a breakfast or dessert item. It is made from a soft, sweet dough that is often filled with whipped cream or ricotta cheese. The dough is shaped into a round or oval shape and is often sprinkled with powdered sugar on top. Maritozzo is a popular treat in central Italy, particularly in the region of Lazio.

15) Panna cotta

Panna cotta is an Italian dessert made from a mixture of heavy cream, sugar, and gelatine, which is then molded and chilled until set. The dessert is typically served with a fruit or chocolate sauce, and is known for its silky, smooth texture and delicate flavor. Panna cotta is a popular dessert in Italy and is served in many Italian restaurants around the world. It is considered a light and refreshing end to a meal and is often paired with a glass of dessert wine.

16) Pasticciotto

Pasticciotto is a traditional Italian pastry that originates from the region of Puglia. It is a shortcrust pastry filled with a creamy custard mixture, similar to a cream pie. Pasticciotto can be served as a dessert or snack, and is often enjoyed with a cappuccino or espresso. The custard filling is flavored with vanilla or lemon zest, and the pastry can be served warm or cold. Pasticciotto is a popular treat in Italy and is widely enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

17) Pastiera Napoletana

Pastiera Napoletana is a traditional Italian dessert that is typically served during the Easter holiday season. It is a sweet, shortcrust pastry filled with a mixture of cooked wheat, ricotta cheese, orange blossom water, and candied fruit. The filling is typically flavored with lemon zest and cinnamon, and the pastry is often dusted with powdered sugar before serving. Pastiera Napoletana is a staple of the Italian Easter holiday and is enjoyed by families and friends as a sweet treat to celebrate the season.

18) Schiacciata Fiorentina

Schiacciata Fiorentina is a traditional Italian cake that originated in Florence. It is a flat, round cake that is typically made with a sweet, yeasted dough and is often filled with fruit or nutella. The cake is usually brushed with olive oil and sprinkled with sugar before baking, which gives it a slightly crunchy texture. Schiacciata Fiorentina is a popular snack or dessert in Tuscany and is often enjoyed with a cappuccino or espresso. It is also sometimes served at special events, such as weddings and birthdays.

19) Sfogliatelle

Sfogliatelle are traditional Italian pastries that are made from a flaky, layered pastry dough filled with a sweet ricotta cheese filling. They are crescent-shaped and have a signature ridged exterior that gives them a unique, shell-like appearance. Sfogliatelle are a staple of Neapolitan cuisine and are enjoyed as a sweet snack or dessert. They are often served with a dusting of powdered sugar and are sometimes filled with a chocolate or fruit filling. Sfogliatelle are widely enjoyed in Italy and are also popular in Italian-American communities.

20) Tartufo di Pizzo

Tartufo di Pizzo is an Italian dessert that originates from the Calabria region. It is a truffle-like confection made from a mixture of chocolate and sugar, which is then coated in cocoa powder or chopped nuts. The name "Tartufo di Pizzo" refers to the shape of the dessert, which resembles a truffle or a small dome, and is often served as a single ball or several smaller balls arranged on a plate. Tartufo di Pizzo is a rich, decadent dessert that is often served as a special treat or as an after-dinner dessert. It is widely enjoyed in Italy and is also popular among chocolate lovers around the world.

21) Tiramisu

Tiramisu is an Italian dessert made from a combination of mascarpone cheese, heavy cream, sugar, and ladyfingers that have been soaked in espresso and liqueur. The dessert is typically layered in a dish, with the mascarpone mixture spread between layers of the soaked ladyfingers. It is often dusted with cocoa powder or shaved chocolate on top, and is chilled until firm. Tiramisu is a popular dessert in Italy and around the world, and is known for its rich, creamy flavor and delicate texture. It is typically served in small portions as a sweet end to a meal or as a special treat.

22) Torrone

Torrone is a traditional Italian nougat that is made from a mixture of honey, sugar, almonds, and egg whites. The ingredients are combined and beaten until they become light and fluffy, and are then poured into a mold to set. Torrone comes in a variety of flavors and textures, from soft and chewy to hard and crunchy. It is often enjoyed as a sweet snack or as a dessert, and is a staple of the Italian holiday season. Torrone is widely available in Italy and in Italian-style confectionery shops around the world.

Torta Caprese is known for its intense chocolate flavor and its moist, dense texture. It is often served as a dessert or as a sweet snack, and is widely enjoyed in Italy and in Italian-style cafes and bakeries around the world.

Torta Sbrisolona is a dense, crunchy cake that is typically served as a sweet snack or as a dessert. Zuccotto is a popular dessert in Italy and is often served as a special treat or at celebrations, such as weddings and holidays.
