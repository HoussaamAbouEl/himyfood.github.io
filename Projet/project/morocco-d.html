1) Almond M'hencha

A classic Moroccan combination of almonds, cinnamon, citrus, and butter, all combined in this one dessert, will leave you wanting more and more.You can also decorate it with nuts and berries for added taste and an appetizing look. Moroccans usually use almonds and cinnamon-like salt and sugar in their cuisines.

2) Sellou

Sellou is a delicious combination of nuts like almonds and ingredients such as sesame, anise, and cinnamon. This delicacy is usually served at special events like childbirth and weddings.It is made in bulk and is loved by the Moroccans and eaten in large quantities to celebrate special occasions.

3) Pistachio, Rose, Rice Pudding

This is a unique blend of colorful and bold flavors that you will never forget. It will become an all-time family favorite dessert recipe soon.Pistachio, rose water, pomegranate and rice are mixed into this bowl of deliciousness.Try it out, and your guests will be begging for the recipe.

4) Honey & Almond Cigars

These Honey & Almond Cigars are a mouth-watering treat and a party pleaser.It takes some time and effort to make these, but the end results are worth all your effort.An excellent dessert for events and gatherings, your guests and family will be awed by the delicious sweetness they offer.

5) Guava Almond Phyllo Cake

This cake is also known as snake cake because of its appearance and snake-like shape.It is made with filo pastry and almond filling; the filo pastry is coiled like a snake, hence the name of this dessert.On top, it is seasoned with citrus to add its fragrance and taste.This sweet dish will not only make your table beautiful but will also taste like a charm.**

6) Walnut Cookies

These Walnut Cookies are popularly known as ghreyba in Morocco and are loved throughout the country.It will only take 20 minutes to prepare these cookies with a rich flavor of walnuts and cinnamon.These are perfect for tea time and for parties.

7) Coconut Cookies

Semolina, coconut, and powdered sugar on top, a treat for your taste buds to savor and appreciate.These are another kind of ghreyba and make a delicious treat for teatime and breakfast.When you try these, you will realize that each variation of Gharib will have something unique to offer.

8) Baghrir

The word “Baghrir” means a pancake with a thousand holes, just like how these look when we make them.These are super soft and moist, and they will start melting as soon as you place them in your mouth.This delicious sweet is as unique as its name and appearance.

9) Chebakia

Chebakia literally means lattice.This is a unique and unforgettable Moroccan sweet dish made by folding dough in unique round spirals that look like a lattice; hence, the dish's name has originated from here.These are then coated with honey and sesame seeds and fried in oil.Try these once, and your tastebuds will never forget the sweet and delicious flavor.

10) Baklava

This dessert is made from puff pastry filled with ground and roasted nuts like almonds and walnuts to get the most exquisite flavors you will always enjoy.This recipe is easy and quick to make, a perfect dessert for dinner and lunch.You can also make variations in the recipe by using different nuts for filling.

11) Sfenj(Moroccan Donuts)

These deep-fried Moroccan donuts turn out super soft, moist, and fresh.You will love the texture of these, and you can also coat them with honey, jam, or chocolate to add to the flavors of these simple and yummy donuts.These won't last a day in your refrigerator because they are yummy and appetizing.

12) Ghariba Cookies

This is a primary and straightforward recipe for traditional Gharib cookies that do not involve fancy ingredients. You can easily make them at home at any time.They have a sandy texture and are generally dry, so some people don't seem to like these.These go great with tea or coffee

13) Kaab ghzal

It will not be wrong to call these the queen of the Moroccan desserts. These are like cones and filled with yummy almond paste that will be a perfect treat for your taste buds.You can serve them for dessert or even as a side dish with your main course because of the mildly sweet flavor.

14) Mint Ice Cream

If you plan to travel to Morocco, be aware because you will find mint in almost everything from tea to cakes to desserts.This Moroccan Mint Ice Cream has a fresh and refreshing flavor that you will start craving more and more.This is a perfect summer treat, simple and easy to make.

15) Carrot & Juicy Orange Salad

Many of you might be wondering that this one is a salad and not a dessert, but its sweet, flavorful taste does the job of a dessert.This salad is juicy and has a super refreshing citrus flavor and tangy taste. You don't want to miss this one.

16) Apple Pie

This inexpensive Apple Pie uses apples, cinnamon, sugar, and phyllo pastry.Most of the ingredients will come from your pantry, so give it a try, and I bet you will definitely love it.Apples are coated with cinnamon and sugar and are packed between the two layers of phyllo pastry to make this perfect sweet dessert that will be loved by all your family and friends.

17) Orange, Pistachio & Honey Cake

This cake is made in a slow cooker, but you will get the same moist texture and yummy flavor from oven cakes.Orange is used abundantly in many desserts in Morocco. This cake also uses orange, but the presence of other ingredients like pistachio and honey will add a unique twist to the usual orange cakes made in Morocco.Try this recipe, and you will be awed by the goodness of different flavors.It will take some time to make these, but the end results are worth all the effort.

18) Almond & Cinnamon Cookies

These cookies have a sweet and mild flavor of almonds and cinnamon. They are a bit different from the traditional ghoribas.This recipe uses oil instead of butter, making these cookies vegan and can be enjoyed by almost everyone.

19) Fekkas

If you like biscotti you will love fekkas. These nutty cookies were made to dunk into warm mint tea. Fekkas are made from a short dough usually incorporating almonds.The cookies will be flavored with great Moroccan spices like anise and orange blossom and studded with dried fruits and roasted nuts. They are baked twice to give this Moroccan dessert a crunchy texture that pairs well with a warm drink.

20) Briouat

Puff pastry is the perfect vessel for wonderfully sweet Moroccan flavors. Briouat are traditional Moroccan pastries that come in sweet and savory forms.When briouat is made sweet warm and nutty fillings like almond paste are often used. The filling will usually have a touch of orange blossom, cinnamon, and anise for a wonderfully complex sweet flavor.

21) Jawhara

If you are craving a truly decadent Moroccan dessert Jawhara is for you. It is an amazing layered pastry that locals love.Jawhara is made from layers of fried warqa, a Moroccan pastry sheet, stacked with velvety cream and roasted almonds. This show-stopping dessert is often paired with local seasonal fruits for a truly lush dessert.

22) Maamoul

Maamoul is a great Moroccan dessert for tea time or with coffee. They are delightfully filled cookies that will surprise you with sweet Moroccan flavors.These Moroccan treats use a sweet almond dough with a touch of cinnamon and orange blossom. Stuffed inside the sweet dough is a delicious date paste. Maamoul is a surprising treat with all the flavors that make desserts in Morocco so irresistible.
